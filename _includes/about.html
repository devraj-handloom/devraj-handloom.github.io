The Maheshwar Legacy

Maheshwari sarees trace their lineage to the 18th century, when Rani Ahilyabai Holkar established the looms of Maheshwar. Our collections honor this heritage with pure silk-cotton blends, geometric motifs, and zari borders handwoven by families who've mastered this craft for generations.

From Thread to Saree

1

Hand-spun yarn

Artisans spin fine cotton and silk yarns by hand, creating the perfect tension for weaving.

2

Warp preparation

Threads are meticulously aligned on traditional pit looms, a process taking 3-4 days.

3

Zari borders

Intricately woven gold and silver motifs frame each saree, hand-drawn by master weavers.

4

Final pallu

The signature pallu with geometric patterns completes each masterpiece (7-10 days total).
